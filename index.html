Biography

Hang Zhang is currently a first year Ph.D. student in Department of Electrical & Computer Engineering at Cornell University. He earned his Master of Philosophy (M.PHIL) degree from the Department of Computer Science & Engineering., The Chinese University of Hong Kong., under the supervision of Prof. Evangeline F. Y. Young. Before that, he received his B. Eng degree from Sichuan University (SCU) in 2015, where he worked with Prof. Xiaohai He.

For his Ph.D. study, he is mostly interested in two major directions: 1) Developing algorithms and theories in artificial intelligence and machine learning especially Bayesian programming and deep learning models with their applications in robotics, computer vision, and quantitative analysis for finance; 2) Develop efficient computing systems or architectures, such as FPGAs, highly-tunable ASICs, for large-scale machine learning applications and high-frequency trading systems. He is also interested in bridging the gap of 1) and 2).

I am alwasy looking for self-motivated undergraduate or Master students (from SCU, CUHK and Cornell) to work on interesting topics/projects related to deep learning, statistical learning, and electronic design automation. If you are interested, please do not hesitate to drop me an email.
